What do you want to accomplish?

After engaging your stakeholders to determine their needs, you will have a clearer understanding of the problem you are trying to solve. Having heard your community's needs, begin to articulate with clarity and trainsparency how the registry will address those needs.

Engaging your community and other stakeholders in defining their needs and interests up front fosters trust that will fuel engagement and long-term retention. Equally as important is laying out the ultimate goals and desired outputs for your stakeholders, likely considering those of the community as the primary ones. Begin by developing an action mission statement related to launching your registry from the lens of several perspectives: your community, your investigators, and your funders. Ultimately this will constitute your organization's goals.
